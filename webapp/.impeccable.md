## Design Context

### Users

**Generators** — renewable energy project operators (primarily in sub-Saharan Africa) who onboard solar/wind/hydro projects, upload verification documentation, and monitor their generation data and R-REC holdings. They log in periodically to check onboarding status and review cumulative MWh output.

**Buyers** — corporate sustainability officers and procurement teams at companies with RE100 or Scope 1/2/3 commitments. They browse available assets, place orders (market/limit/stop/stop-limit), and track progress toward emissions reduction goals. They may use this daily during active procurement cycles.

**Admins** — REX staff who approve onboarding submissions, review and execute pending orders via paper contract, and create user accounts. Usage is task-driven, not habitual.

One company can hold both generator and buyer roles. Accounts are admin-created only — no self-serve signup.

The platform is used during business hours, primarily on desktop, by professionals already fluent with financial tools. It is not a consumer app.

### Brand Personality

**Three words: authoritative · trustworthy · bank-like**

REX is a regulated-feeling commodity exchange — closer to Chase Bank's trade portal or the Gold Standard registry than to a crypto dashboard or a sustainability marketing site. The interface should feel like it was built for institutions, not for retail users. Precision and restraint signal credibility. Visual warmth comes only from the brand palette — not from rounded corners, friendly illustrations, or encouraging microcopy.

REX's environmental mission is expressed through what the platform *does* (verified carbon accounting, blockchain traceability, African renewable energy) — not through how it looks. The UI should not use green leafy motifs, planet icons, or nature photography as decoration.

### Aesthetic Direction

**Tone**: Institutional precision — a Bloomberg data terminal crossed with a private banking portal. Dense when the data demands it, never cluttered. Every element justified by function.

**Theme**: System-adaptive (respects prefers-color-scheme). Light mode uses a near-white background with a barely perceptible teal tint (`oklch(98.5% 0.006 195)`), not pure white. Dark mode uses a deep ink, not black. Both modes share the same teal/orange accent system.

**Color palette** (OKLCH):
- Brand teal (primary): `oklch(38% 0.09 195)` — deep, authoritative, from the R-REC Standard PDF
- Brand orange (accent): `oklch(68% 0.17 52)` — warm amber, from the REX logo
- Surface light: `oklch(98.5% 0.006 195)` — white with teal ghost
- Surface dark: `oklch(14% 0.015 195)` — deep ink with teal hint
- Text primary (light): `oklch(18% 0.02 195)`
- Text secondary (light): `oklch(48% 0.02 195)`
- Text primary (dark): `oklch(94% 0.01 195)`
- Text secondary (dark): `oklch(62% 0.015 195)`
- Border (light): `oklch(88% 0.012 195)`
- Border (dark): `oklch(28% 0.02 195)`

Teal is dominant (60%); neutral surfaces carry it as a ghost tint. Orange is the 10% accent — used sparingly for CTAs, active states, and key data highlights only.

**Typography**:
- Display / headings: **Bricolage Grotesque** (Google Fonts) — structured geometry with quiet authority; reads well at large sizes without looking tech-startup
- Body / UI text: **Barlow** (Google Fonts) — highly legible, slightly condensed, efficient at small sizes, excellent for tabular data and form labels; the Semi Condensed weight works well for dashboard labels

**References**:
- Gold Standard registry (clean institutional data presentation)
- Chase Bank Trade portal (structured, task-oriented, zero decorative noise)

**Anti-references**:
- Crypto dashboards (no dark mode with neon accents, no "connected wallet" aesthetics)
- Greenwashing marketing pages (no hero gradients, no leaf/sun/planet icons used decoratively)

### Design Principles

1. **Data before decoration** — charts, tables, and numbers are the product. Typography and spacing serve them; they never compete.
2. **Institutional restraint** — if a visual element doesn't carry information or establish hierarchy, remove it. Trust is built by what isn't there.
3. **Environmental credibility through function** — the platform's environmental story is in the data (MWh verified, tCO2e retired, blockchain hashes). The UI doesn't need to remind users it's "green."
4. **Precision at every scale** — tight alignment, consistent spacing tokens, no rounding corners to seem friendly. Precision is the brand.
5. **Role-appropriate density** — admin views can be dense (data tables, submission queues); buyer/generator dashboards prioritize clarity and scannable hierarchy. Adapt density to task frequency, not to a single template.
